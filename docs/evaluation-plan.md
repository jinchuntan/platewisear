# PlateNudge — Evaluation Plan

This document outlines a simple evaluation plan suitable for a short academic AR project report. Results must be filled in after actual testing is conducted.

---

## Objectives

1. Assess whether PlateNudge effectively supports **awareness** of food waste and SDG 12.
2. Evaluate the **usability** of the AR interface and decision-making flow.
3. Measure **behavioural intention** — whether users report increased willingness to reduce food waste after using the app.

---

## Participants

- **Number:** 5 to 10 participants.
- **Demographics:** University students or peers (convenience sample).
- **Recruitment:** Voluntary participation; informed consent obtained.

---

## Procedure

1. **Briefing** (2 minutes) — Explain the purpose of the study and obtain consent.
2. **Pre-test questionnaire** — Baseline awareness and attitudes (see below).
3. **Task-based testing** (5–10 minutes):
   - Open PlateNudge on a mobile device.
   - Print or display the marker.
   - Start the AR experience and scan the marker.
   - Browse at least 3 facts.
   - Try all four food-waste actions.
   - Complete the quiz and pledge.
4. **Post-test questionnaire** — Awareness, usability, and behavioural intention (see below).
5. **Debriefing** (1 minute) — Thank participant and answer questions.

---

## Pre-Test Questions (Awareness Baseline)

| # | Question | Scale |
|---|----------|-------|
| 1 | How aware are you of SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production)? | 1 (Not at all) – 5 (Very aware) |
| 2 | How often do you think about food waste in your daily life? | 1 (Never) – 5 (Always) |
| 3 | Do you currently take any actions to reduce food waste? | Yes / No / Sometimes |

---

## Post-Test Questions

### Awareness

| # | Question | Scale |
|---|----------|-------|
| 4 | After using PlateNudge, how aware are you of SDG 12? | 1 (Not at all) – 5 (Very aware) |
| 5 | Did you learn something new about food waste from this app? | Yes / No |
| 6 | Which fact was most surprising to you? | Open-ended |

### Usability

| # | Question | Scale |
|---|----------|-------|
| 7 | The app was easy to use. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |
| 8 | The AR experience worked reliably with the marker. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |
| 9 | The action buttons and feedback were clear and understandable. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |
| 10 | The quiz questions were relevant and appropriate. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |
| 11 | What would you improve about the app? | Open-ended |

### Behavioural Intention

| # | Question | Scale |
|---|----------|-------|
| 12 | After using PlateNudge, I am more likely to save leftovers. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |
| 13 | After using PlateNudge, I am more likely to share surplus food. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |
| 14 | After using PlateNudge, I am more likely to compost unavoidable scraps. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |
| 15 | After using PlateNudge, I feel more motivated to reduce food waste. | 1 (Strongly disagree) – 5 (Strongly agree) |

---

## Data Analysis

- Compare pre-test and post-test awareness scores (paired comparison).
- Calculate average usability ratings.
- Summarise behavioural intention scores.
- Report open-ended feedback thematically.

---

## Important Notes

- This evaluation plan is for a small-scale academic prototype study.
- Results must be collected and filled in after actual testing with real participants.
- The app does not claim to have changed real behaviour — results measure self-reported awareness and intention.
- Follow your institution's ethics and consent requirements.
