About PlateWise AR

Project overview, SDG 12 alignment, and sources

Purpose

PlateWise AR is an educational prototype that uses browser-based Augmented Reality to help users explore how everyday food-waste decisions connect to environmental impact, household responsibility, and sustainable behaviour.

The application is designed as a short academic AR project. It does not claim to have already changed real user behaviour — instead it is intended to support awareness, reflection, and behavioural intention around food waste and SDG 12.

SDG 12 Alignment

Sustainable Development Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production calls for sustainable consumption and production patterns. Target 12.3 specifically aims to halve per-capita global food waste at retail and consumer levels by 2030.

PlateWise AR supports SDG 12 by presenting food-waste statistics, visualising the consequences of disposal decisions, and encouraging users to reflect on actions such as saving leftovers, sharing surplus, and composting unavoidable scraps.

Assignment Components

1. AR Visualization Layer

The app overlays digital assets onto the physical environment using marker-based AR. Content includes a 3D plate, food pile, action visuals (bin, container, compost, share), spatial text with food-waste statistics, and an SDG 12 label — all built from simple A-Frame primitives.

2. User Interaction Schema

Interactive UI elements allow users to navigate educational content and simulate decision-making. Users browse facts with Previous/Next buttons and choose one of four food-waste actions (Throw Away, Save Leftovers, Share, Compost). Each action updates the AR scene and provides written feedback explaining its environmental implications.

3. Educational and Empirical Content Integration

The app integrates meaningful data, statistical insights, and actionable information to improve understanding of SDG 12 and encourage responsible food-waste behaviour. Facts and statistics are drawn from official sources and presented in a concise, user-friendly format. A quiz and reflection section reinforces learning.

Empirical Facts Used in the App

  • 1.05 billion tonnes of food were wasted globally in 2022.
  • Households accounted for about 60% of global food waste.
  • About 19% of food available to consumers was wasted across retail, food service, and households.
  • SDG 12.3 aims to halve per-capita global food waste at retail and consumer levels by 2030.
  • Food waste represents embedded resources: land, water, labour, transport, energy, and packaging.
  • Composting is useful for unavoidable scraps, but edible food should be eaten, saved, or shared first.
  • Household decisions matter because many food-waste actions happen after food reaches the consumer.

Limitations

  • The app uses marker-based AR with the standard Hiro marker, not real food recognition.
  • No AI features in this version. AI tips, image scanning, object recognition, and any backend/ML are intentionally out of scope and planned as future work — this is the core marker-based version.
  • This prototype is educational and does not measure actual household food-waste reduction unless user evaluation is later conducted.
  • Statistics are simplified for learning and should be cited properly in the report.
  • Camera-based AR requires HTTPS or localhost.
